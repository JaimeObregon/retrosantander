The Aranzadi Science Society preserves the Jesús Elósegui Irazusta Photographic Collection, digitised through an agreement with the Benedictines of Lazkao. The process brought together thousands of images on different media.

Each photograph was assigned a code and description, using the original inventory and contributions from relatives and collaborators. The collection is both a tribute to Elósegui, a founder of Aranzadi, and an open resource for the public.

Born in Tolosa in 1907, he became a professional photographer in 1932 and pursued interests in sport, culture and natural sciences. He worked with José Miguel de Barandiaran, studied dolmens and mines in Aralar, and published in journals such as Munibe.

The collection is released under a Creative Commons BY-SA license, compatible with Wikipedia.

It is a photographic archive of great historical and scientific value, available to all.
