Juan San Martín (Eibar, 1922 — Hondarribia, 2005) devoted his life to Basque culture, taking part in literary, artistic and social activities, and collaborating with figures such as Barandiarán, Caro Baroja and Oteiza.

He served as secretary and vice-president of Euskaltzaindia, promoted literacy campaigns and the creation of ikastolas, and edited the magazine Egan. As a poet, he modernised Basque lyricism alongside Aresti, Azurmendi and Lasa.

In 1989 he became the first Ararteko, carrying out intense social and cultural work. He was also director of Culture of the Basque Government and took part in several academic and literary institutions.

A mountaineering enthusiast, he climbed in the Pyrenees, Picos de Europa and Alps, and joined speleological expeditions. He wrote in numerous publications and discovered archaeological sites and works of art.

His photographic collection is preserved in the General Archive of Gipuzkoa and made available under a Creative Commons BY-SA license, compatible with Wikipedia.
